🌐 Custom Domain Sending

Send emails from you@yourcompany.com instead of a Gmail or Yahoo address. This improves deliverability and looks more professional.

📚 How to set up custom domain email

Step 1: Get a business email

You need an email address on your own domain (e.g. hello@yourcompany.com). Popular options:

  • Google Workspace ($6/mo) — Uses Gmail interface, supports App Passwords
  • Microsoft 365 ($6/mo) — Uses Outlook interface
  • Zoho Mail (free tier available) — Good budget option
  • Your hosting provider — Many hosts include email with your domain

Step 2: Set up DNS records

Add these records in your domain registrar (Namecheap, GoDaddy, Cloudflare, etc.):

RecordPurposeWhat it does
SPF TXT record Tells servers which IPs can send from your domain
DKIM TXT record Digitally signs your emails to prove authenticity
DMARC TXT record Policy that tells receivers how to handle unauthenticated emails

Your email provider (Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, etc.) will give you the exact values to add.

Step 3: Connect to MachReach

Once your domain email is set up, add it to MachReach just like any other account:

  1. Click "+ Add Email Account" above
  2. Enter your custom domain email (e.g. hello@yourcompany.com)
  3. If using Google Workspace, it auto-detects Gmail settings
  4. For other providers, open Advanced Settings and enter your IMAP/SMTP details
  5. Use an App Password if your provider supports it
💡 Pro tip: Start with a low sending volume (10-20 emails/day) for the first 2 weeks to warm up your domain. This builds reputation and prevents your emails from landing in spam.

🛡 Email Deliverability Check

Verify your domain's SPF, DKIM, and DMARC records. All three are required by Gmail and Yahoo since 2024 for bulk senders.
